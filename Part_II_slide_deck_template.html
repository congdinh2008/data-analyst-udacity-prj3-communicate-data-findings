Ride Duration and Distance Distributions

Ride durations exhibit a positive (right) skew, averaging 621 seconds (10.35 min) across 181,382 observations. Three-quarters of rides conclude within 784 seconds (13.07 min).

Similarly, trip distances are positively (right) skewed, with an average of ~1.68 km. Seventy-five percent of trips cover distances within 2.22 km.

def plot_trip_duration_distribution(df, duration_column, duration_cutoff, bins_step, figsize=(11.69, 8.27)):
    sns.set_style("whitegrid")
    plt.figure(figsize=figsize)
    
    # Create a histogram of trip duration
    bins = np.arange(60, duration_cutoff, bins_step)
    plt.hist(data=df.query(f"{duration_column} < {duration_cutoff}"), x=duration_column, bins=bins)
    
    # Set the labels and title
    plt.xlabel('Trip Duration (sec)')
    plt.ylabel('Count')
    plt.title('Distribution of Trip Duration in Second')
    plt.tight_layout()
    plt.show()

Rentals on Age, Gender & User Type

GoBike rentals require users to be at least 18 years old, with 75% being younger than 39. The 31-year-old age group has the highest rental count, exceeding 10,000 rides, indicating popularity among young riders.

Male users lead in GoBike usage, recording ~130,000 rides, while females account for ~40,000 rides. Other gender categories contribute to a few thousand rides.

Two user types exist: "Subscribers" (paying monthly or yearly) and "Customers" (paying per trip or day). Over 89.2% of rides come from subscribers, with customers making up 10.9% of the rental records.

def plot_user_type_distribution(df, user_type_column, figsize=(11.69, 8.27)):
    # Calculate user type ratios
    customer_ratio = df.query(f'{user_type_column} == "Customer"').shape[0] / df.shape[0]
    subscriber_ratio = df.query(f'{user_type_column} == "Subscriber"').shape[0] / df.shape[0]
    
    sns.set_style("whitegrid")
    pie_data = [customer_ratio, subscriber_ratio]
    fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=figsize)
    
    # Create a pie chart of user type
    plt.pie(pie_data, explode=[0, 0.1], labels=['Customer', 'Subscriber'], startangle=90, autopct='%1.1f%%', shadow=True)
    
    plt.title('User Type Distribution')
    plt.tight_layout()
    plt.show()

Rentals of Each Day of Week on Different User Types

Subscribers dominate GoBike rentals, particularly on weekdays, indicating a commuting trend with over 10,000 more rides on weekdays compared to weekends. This suggests a strong weekday reliance, possibly for commuting purposes.

In contrast, customer rentals remain relatively stable throughout the week, totaling 3,000 to 4,000 rides each day. This consistent pattern may indicate that customer rentals, fewer in number, are likely contributed by tourists exploring the Bay area.

On weekdays, the average trip distance ranges from 1.7 to 1.8 km, exceeding that of weekends (1.6 to 1.7 km). However, the pattern is reversed for trip duration. On weekdays, users embark on shorter trips lasting 10 to 12 minutes, while on weekends, the average duration extends to 13 to 18 minutes.

Average Ride Duration and Distance by Region

In San Francisco, there were 133,708 rentals, 41,434 in Oakland, and 8,073 in San Jose. The average ride duration across the regions peaks around noon, with San Francisco riders taking the longest trips (over 10 minutes), followed by Oakland (~9.2 min) and San Jose (~8.3 min).

Similarly, the average ride distance shows a rush-hour pattern, with peaks at around 8 am and 5 pm. San Francisco riders cover the longest distance (averaging over 1.5 km), followed by Oakland (~1.4 km) and San Jose (~1.2 km). This aligns with expectations based on city size and population.
